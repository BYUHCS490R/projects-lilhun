About Irenland

Ireland is an island in northwestern Europe and the second-largest island in the British Isles. It spans 32,595 square miles and is divided into two parts. Most of the island is the sovereign Republic of Ireland, while the smaller northeastern region is Northern Ireland, which is part of the United Kingdom (Ireland — Wikipedia).

Today, Ireland has a lively landscape steeped in tradition and rich history. It is home to beautiful national parks, bustling cities like Dublin and Limerick, and medieval towns such as Kilkenny and Galway. Vast castles and quiet ruins lie scattered across grassy plains, steep hills, and rugged cliffs, often tucked away in hidden corners waiting to be discovered. Towering sea cliffs and dramatic basalt columns shape the coastline, creating some of the most striking natural scenery in Europe.

History...

People have lived in Ireland since prehistoric times, but the culture became distinctly Celtic when migrating Celtic groups arrived from mainland Europe during the Bronze Age. During the Middle Ages, Ireland was not a unified kingdom but a collection of many smaller ones. The Viking Age (c. 800–1100 AD) had a major impact as Vikings raided monasteries, settled, traded, and founded important cities such as Dublin, Limerick, Cork, Wexford, and Waterford. Over time, they blended into Irish society. England also played a major role in Irish history through centuries of conflict and still controls part of the island today.(Ireland — Wikipedia)
